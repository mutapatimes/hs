Using Halia.

Your store's connected and your customers are graded. Here's how to turn that into happier VICs and repeat business, in a few minutes a week.

On this page

Your dashboard

The dashboard shows every customer, graded, with your potential VICs at the top.

Each person comes with the reasons behind their grade, a prime postcode, a work email at a known firm, a quiet pattern of high-value orders, so you can see at a glance why they're worth your attention. You can sort, filter by grade, and open anyone to see the detail.

What the grades mean

Every customer gets a grade from A* down to C. Think of it as “how likely is this person a high-value client worth a personal touch.”

  • A*Your strongest signals of real wealth and intent. Reach out personally.
  • AClear high-value signs. Worth a personal note or an invite.
  • BPromising, some good signals. Keep an eye on them.
  • CNo strong signals yet. Most of your list sits here.

A grade is a prompt for a human, never an automatic decision. It only ever suggests giving someone more attention, it never changes prices, offers, or what a customer can buy.

Reaching out

Found someone worth a hello? Halia helps you send a message that feels personal, without the blank-page moment.

  1. Pick a client (or a group)

    Open a customer from your dashboard, or select several to reach out to together.

  2. Choose a draft

    Halia offers a few warm templates or a personal note. Edit it however you like, it's your voice.

  3. Send it your way

    Send from your own inbox or WhatsApp, or build a segment and send from your email tool. Halia never sends for you.

Connected an email tool? Halia can drop your chosen group straight into Klaviyo, Mailchimp, or HubSpot as a ready-to-send list.

Real-time alerts

You don't have to keep checking the dashboard. When a VIC places an order, Halia can tell you right away.

Turn on a browser notification and email alert, and, if you've connected Slack, a message to your team channel. A quick, well-timed thank-you after a big order is one of the simplest ways to turn a good customer into a loyal one.

The Orders page

Every order, newest first, ranked by the client behind it, so the ones worth a personal touch rise to the top.

Each row shows the date, the client with their grade, the order number, its status, and the amount. The stats above the list track the order value in view, how many need fulfilling, and how many are already fulfilled, so you can see the day's shape at a glance.

  1. Filter to what matters

    Show only clients graded above C, or every grade. Narrow by status (unfulfilled, fulfilled, refunded, cancelled), by date range, or search by name.

  2. Open an order

    Click any row to see the full order and the client behind it, their grade, the reasons for it, and their history with you.

  3. Act while it's live

    Each order offers a ready message that matches its status, a warm thank-you on a fresh order, a note when it ships. Send it from your own inbox or WhatsApp.

A well-timed thank-you after a big order is one of the simplest ways to turn a good customer into a loyal one. The Orders page is built for exactly that moment.

Catalogues

Turn a hand-picked selection of products into a private catalogue you can send to one client. Available once your Shopify store is connected.

  1. Build it

    In the Catalogues tab, create a catalogue, pick the products, and add a personal cover line. Save it and Halia builds both formats for you.

  2. Two ways to share

    Open PDF gives you a print-ready catalogue with an Enquire link on every product. Open form is an interactive page where the client ticks what they'd like; their request is emailed straight to you.

  3. Send the link

    Copy the PDF or form link and send it your way, or set a catalogue as active and drop {catalog_link} into any client-email template, Halia inserts a link personalised to that client.

Every catalogue is private to the client it's sent to. It carries no Halia branding and never shows the client's own details back to them.

Campaign monitoring

Give a piece of outreach a name and a window, add the clients you're targeting, then watch, in one screen, exactly what sales it drove.

Set it up

  1. Create the campaign

    In the Campaigns tab, give it a name and set a start and end date. That window is what every number is measured against.

  2. Add the clients

    On the Clients tab, tick the people you're targeting, use the filters to find them fast, and choose Add to campaign. Add more at any time; editing the name or dates never clears who's in it.

  3. Open the monitor

    Click a campaign to open its monitor as a full page inside the app.

What the monitor shows

Six headline figures sit across the top:

  • RevenueTotal sales from campaign members inside the window.
  • In campaignHow many clients you added.
  • BoughtHow many of them actually ordered.
  • ConversionBought as a share of members.
  • OrdersNumber of orders placed in the window.
  • Avg orderAverage value of those orders.

Below that, the detail:

  1. Came back from gone quiet

    The stand-out number: clients who had gone silent (no order for 90 days before the window) and bought again during the campaign, and the revenue you recovered by bringing them back.

  2. Sales over time

    A week-by-week chart of revenue across the window, so you can see when the campaign landed.

  3. Revenue by signal and by grade

    Which kinds of client drove the sales, by the signal that surfaced them (work email, prime postcode, and so on) and by their Halia grade.

  4. Top clients

    The members who spent the most in the window, with their grade, order count, and revenue.

Save the whole screen as a PDF from the button at the top, handy for a team review or a note to the buyer.

Every figure is computed live from your own orders each time you open the monitor. Halia stores only the campaign's name, dates, and an anonymous id for each member, nothing about the customers themselves.

Latent value

Latent value is a client's projected worth once you've nurtured them into a top client, not what they've spent so far.

It grows their current spend toward your ceiling, weighted by their Halia score, so a high-scoring modest spender shows their real upside. Your ceiling is your highest lifetime client, or average order value × the most orders from one client, whichever is higher. Tune the numbers behind it in Settings.

A simple weekly rhythm

Most teams get the value in a few minutes a week:

  1. Skim the top of your list

    Check your newest A* and A clients and anyone who has just moved up.

  2. Send two or three personal notes

    A short, genuine message to your best clients goes a long way. Halia drafts it; you send it.

  3. Let alerts handle the rest

    When a VIC orders during the week, you'll hear about it, reach out while it's fresh.

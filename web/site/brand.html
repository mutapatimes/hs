Brand

The Halia brand.

Halia surfaces the quiet, high-value clients hiding in a brand's own data. The brand should feel the same way it works: calm, precise, and quietly premium. This page holds the mark, the colours, the type, and the voice.

The name

Halia.

Halia was a nymph of the sea. The name suits the idea at the centre of the product: your most valuable clients are already there, below the surface, and Halia brings them up into the light. The tone follows from that, unhurried, deep, and sure of itself.

Always written Halia, capital H, never in all caps, never abbreviated.

The mark

A single point of light.

An eight-point star: the one client worth more than all the rest, found and set apart. It pairs with the Halia wordmark set in Cormorant Garamond, or stands alone as an app icon.

Do
  • Give the mark clear space of at least its own height on every side.
  • Use the cream, near-black, or warm-gold versions only.
  • Keep the wordmark in Cormorant Garamond, weight 300.
  • Let it sit small and confident; it does not need to shout.
Do not
  • Recolour the mark or add gradients, shadows, or outlines.
  • Stretch, rotate, or rebuild the star from different proportions.
  • Set the wordmark in another typeface or in all caps.
  • Place it on a busy image without a calm, solid panel behind it.

Typography

A serif for the idea, a sans for the detail.

Voice

Calm, plain, and quietly optimistic.

Halia speaks like a trusted advisor, not a growth tool. It is precise without being technical, warm without being soft, and it never hypes. The reader should feel that real value, already theirs, is about to be found.

  • Plain over clever. Say the useful thing simply.
  • Show the evidence. Name the signal, the score, the number.
  • Lead with the client's gain, not the mechanics.
  • Reassure on privacy in our own words: we read only your data, never store it, never share it.
  • No hype, no growth-hacking tone, no exclamation marks.
  • No jargon where a plain word will do.
  • Never imply we see other brands' data, or learn from yours.
  • No em dashes. Use a comma, a colon, or a full stop.
